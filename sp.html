Description

Umap plot of Shakespeare text

Method

  • Text was gathered from kaggle dataset https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/kingburrito666/shakespeare-plays
  • Spacy en_core_web_lg model was used for tokenization and embeddings
  • Text was tokenized and cleaned for stopwords and symbols in the text
  • Embeddings were generated and plotted with whatlies library

Results

Two main correlations are found, separating modern words from more antiquated words.
Modern examples: languish, dower, leper, etc.
Antiquated examples: inheritrix, sleepest, shoon, thisn, etc.
The selected model could be used to target words that need more explanation for modern readers and performers.

One major outlier group was found, which seems to be composed of words ending in an apostrophe. e.g. howso', whene', howsome', howe', etc.
This seems to be a result of an issue during stemming or tokenization, as it seems these should be contractions of [word] and 'ever.'
Regardless, that these are outliers from other contractions is notable.

Other notable groupings:

  • ordinal numbers
  • French words
  • animals
  • familial words (mother,father, etc.)
  • ordinal directions
  • -ion and -ment suffix words
